Growth and Future of Esports

How did esports become increasingly popular?

In more concrete terms, eSports are competitive video gaming that is frequently organized by various leagues, ladders, and tournaments.

Players typically participate in eSports as members of teams or other "sporting" organizations that are supported by different business organizations.

Professional gamers have developed their own breed of celebrities thanks to streaming services like Twitch and YouTube, establishing devoted fan following, this is one of the main factors that has contributed to the rise of esports.

What is the potenial future of esports?

What has changed in esports

Esports "naturally arose when video games started to incorporate network capabilities and the opportunity to play against others both in person and over the world."

This marked the transition from player-versus-machine gaming to player-versus-player gaming.

Future in the esports industry.

Millions of people watch live event streams, billions of dollars are made from high-profile events, and tens of thousands of supporters fill arenas across the world (Reyes, 2019).

There are currently more than 2.6 billion players worldwide (Gough, 2019), and according to industry analysts, the esports business has a total commercial value of $138 billion as of 2018.

The esports ecosystem, which includes games, competitions, streaming services, and related commercial ventures, was also claimed to have generated $1 billion in income for the first time in 2019 and is projected to generate close to $2 billion in yearly sales by 2022. (Reyes, 2019).
