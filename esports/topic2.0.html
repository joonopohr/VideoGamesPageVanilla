Esports

What is a esports?

Video game competitions are known as esports, or electronic sports.

What is the purpose of esports?

In order to build a community and social network, esports teams up individual gamers in one location. Teams are able to compete in organised video game tournaments, most of which are held online. With the shared goal of winning a game, several players partner up to compete against an opposing team. It calls for effort, practise, and the development of skills.

How Esports fuels technological advancements related towards gaming.?

The development of technology has opened up new gaming and player experiences. In example, improvements in faster and better internet connectivity allowed local gaming communities to establish LAN connections between devices to engage in multiplayer computer game competition.

Esports and the economics surrounding it?

There are millions of people who watch live event streams, billions of dollars are made from high-profile events, and tens of thousands of supporters fill arenas across the world (Reyes, 2019). There are currently more than 2.6 billion players worldwide (Gough, 2019), and according to industry analysts, the esports business has a total commercial value of $138 billion as of 2018

"Esports will rival the biggest traditional sports leagues in terms of future oppurtunities"

- Steve Bornstein, former CEO of ESPN and NFL Network and current chairman of Activision Blizzard esports divison
