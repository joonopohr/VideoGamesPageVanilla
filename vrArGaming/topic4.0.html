AR & VR Gaming

Introduction

This incredible technology has seen fiction come into reality, as once the imaginary idea of fully experiencing a virtual world is ever closer to being realized in full. The main focus of current virtual reality technology is recreating the visual aspect of experiencing a world, as well as the audio. Augmented reality is similar to virtual reality in aspect of experiencing a virtual world, but instead of becoming part of a virtual world, augmented reality brings the virtual world into the real world.

Virtual Reality

Virtual reality is usually experienced by putting on a virtual reality headset. If the wearer wishes to play a game using the VR headset, the player must have some input into the game, which can be done using different input devices, like a console controller, keyboard and mouse, or vr touch controllers. The headset covers the player’s entire vision, allowing him/her to experience a virtual 3D world.

Augmented reality

Augmented reality games use reality as the setting, and adds it’s own virtual layer to the world. Games like the hit game Pokémon Go uses the player’s surroundings as the environment for the game, which has it’s upsides and downsides, as this, like virtual reality, will most likely involve movement, and can present dangers to the player that are not present on other game platforms where the player remains seated or in one small area.

More Immersion

Different technologies like spatial awareness increases the immersive experience of virtual reality.
